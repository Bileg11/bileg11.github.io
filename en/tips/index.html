FOR STUDENTS
Coming to Shanghai
as a student
  • Apply to Fudan, Tongji, SJTU universities
  • Chinese language courses (HSK prep) — in-house at most universities
  • Student visa (X1, X2) — issued for your study duration
  • Affordable areas: Putuo, Baoshan — cheap food, fast internet
  • WeChat — Mongolian phone numbers work for registration
  • Alipay — register with foreign passport since 2024
  • University canteen (食堂) — very cheap, ¥10–20/meal
  • Dormitory booking arranged with admission

Visa & Preparation
Visa & Preparation
  • Mongolian citizens can enter China visa-free for up to 30 days. Passport must be valid for 6+ months.
  • Always get travel insurance — hospitals in China are expensive
  • Start preparing 2–4 weeks before your trip
Money & Payment
Money & Payment
  • Use WeChat Pay — the most widely accepted payment in Shanghai
  • Carry some cash yuan — smaller markets may not take cards
  • Tax refund available on purchases over ¥500
  • Foreign card ATM withdrawals carry additional bank fees
  • Major malls accept Alipay, UnionPay cards
Language & Communication
Language & Communication
  • No Chinese needed — Google Translate Camera is very handy
  • DeepL translates more accurately than Google
  • Staff in shops and restaurants often don't speak English — show text on screen
  • Taxis: use DiDi app, not Uber — cheaper, reliable
  • Save your destination in Chinese characters to show drivers easily
VPN & Internet
VPN & Internet
  • Google, Facebook, Instagram are blocked in China
  • Download and configure VPN while still in Mongolia — hard to do in China
  • Recommended: NordVPN, ExpressVPN — reliable and fast
  • Use a Chinese SIM (China Unicom) or Mongolian roaming
  • Hotel WiFi is usually fast — VPN works well on it
Metro & Transport
Metro & Transport
  • Shanghai metro has 20 lines and 500+ stations — world's largest
  • Get a Jiaotong card — ¥20 deposit, works on all lines & buses
  • From Pudong airport: Maglev (fast, ¥50) or Metro Line 2 (cheap, ¥8)
  • From Hongqiao airport: Line 2 or Line 10 to city center
  • Fares: ¥3–15 depending on distance
  • Key lines: Line 2 (airport↔city), Line 1 (People's Square), Line 10 (old city)
Safety
Safety
  • Shanghai is a very safe city — theft is rare, crime is uncommon
  • Stay alert in crowded areas (Nanjing Road)
  • Carry a passport photocopy separately — leave original in hotel
  • Your passport is required at hospitals — always carry it
  • Emergencies: 110 (police), 120 (ambulance)
Weather & Clothing
Weather & Clothing
  • Summer (Jun–Aug): Hot and humid — light clothing, sun protection, water bottle
  • Autumn (Sep–Nov): Most pleasant — best time to visit Shanghai
  • Winter (Dec–Feb): Cold and wet — warm base layers needed
  • Spring (Mar–May): Humid and rainy — bring a raincoat

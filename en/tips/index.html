Important

Visa-Free Entry for Mongolians

Mongolian citizens get 30 days visa-free entry into China. Just bring your valid passport — no visa needed for tourism.

  • Passport must be valid 6+ months
  • Return ticket recommended
  • Extensions possible at local PSB office
  • Stay strictly within 30 days to avoid fines
💳

Money & Payment

China is nearly cashless. Set up mobile payments before you arrive.

  • Alipay: Now accepts international Visa/Mastercard — easiest option
  • WeChat Pay: Requires Chinese bank account for full features
  • Cash: Carry 200–500¥ as backup
  • Exchange rate: 1¥ ≈ 535₮ (check current rate)
  • ATMs at airports accept foreign cards
🗣

Language

Very few people speak English outside hotels and tourist areas. Prepare ahead:

  • Download Google Translate (offline mode + camera translate)
  • Save key phrases in Chinese characters on your phone
  • Use translation apps at restaurants and markets
  • Show your destination in writing — don't rely on spoken Chinese
  • Our guide handles all communication for you 🇲🇳
🔒

VPN & Internet

Google, Instagram, Facebook, and most Western apps are blocked in China. Install a VPN before you arrive.

  • NordVPN — most reliable, works in China
  • ExpressVPN — fast and easy to use
  • Install and activate VPN while still in Mongolia
  • You cannot download VPNs from Chinese app stores
  • Hotel WiFi + VPN = most stable combo
🚇

Getting Around

Shanghai's metro is world-class — clean, fast, and cheap.

  • Buy a transit card (交通卡) or use Alipay QR
  • Most rides cost 3–6¥ within the city
  • DiDi (滴滴) = China's Uber — download before arriving
  • Metro runs 6:00 AM – 11:00 PM roughly
  • See our full Metro Guide for details
🛡

Safety

Shanghai is extremely safe. Crime rates are very low.

  • Keep your passport and a copy separate
  • Use official taxis or DiDi — avoid unofficial drivers at airports
  • Pickpockets rare but possible in crowded areas (Yuyuan, Nanjing Rd)
  • Emergency number: 110 (police), 120 (ambulance)
  • Register at your hotel (required by law)
🌤

Weather & Best Time to Visit

Shanghai has four distinct seasons.

  • Spring (Mar–May): Best weather, 15–22°C, cherry blossoms
  • Summer (Jun–Aug): Hot and humid, 30–38°C, occasional typhoons
  • Autumn (Sep–Nov): Excellent — 15–25°C, clear skies ⭐
  • Winter (Dec–Feb): Cold and damp, 3–10°C
  • Avoid Chinese national holidays — massive crowds
🏨

Accommodation Tips

Shanghai has hotels across all budgets.

  • Budget: 150–250¥/night (hostel or economy hotel)
  • Mid-range: 400–800¥/night
  • Luxury: 1,000¥+/night (InterContinental, Waldorf Astoria)
  • Stay in Puxi (west side) for easier access to most attractions
  • We can book hotels for you — 50¥ service fee
📸

Must-Have Apps

Download these before leaving Mongolia:

  • Alipay — payments everywhere
  • DiDi — taxi/rideshare
  • Google Translate (with offline CN pack)
  • NordVPN or ExpressVPN
  • Maps.me — offline Shanghai maps
  • Trip.com — hotels, train tickets, flights

Student Visa (X Visa)

  • X1 visa: Long-term study (6+ months)
  • X2 visa: Short-term study (under 6 months)
  • Requires: admission letter + JW201/202 form from your university
  • Convert X2 to residence permit within 30 days of arrival
  • Health check required at designated Shanghai hospitals
📱

WeChat & Alipay Setup

  • WeChat: needs Chinese bank card for full Pay features
  • Alipay: easier — links to international card
  • Open a bank account at Bank of China or ICBC with your student ID + passport + residence permit
  • Bank account unlocks WeChat Pay and other services
🏠

Finding Housing

  • On-campus dorms: most affordable option (800–1,500¥/month)
  • Off-campus: 2,000–5,000¥/month for a room
  • Use Ziroom (自如) or 58.com for apartments
  • We can help you navigate the rental process in Chinese

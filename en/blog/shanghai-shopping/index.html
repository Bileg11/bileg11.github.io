Shanghai Shopping Guide — Best Markets, Malls & What to Buy in 2026

May 2026 · 6 min read · LFS Shanghai
01 — Overview
Why Shanghai Is One of Asia's Best Shopping Cities

Shanghai offers everything from luxury global brands to rock-bottom wholesale markets. Most shopping districts are directly accessible by metro, making it easy to hop between them.

  • Markets: negotiating is expected — start at 30–50% of the asking price
  • Malls: fixed prices, but sales are common especially on weekdays
  • Pay with WeChat Pay or Alipay everywhere — almost no one takes cash or foreign cards
  • A Mongolian guide can negotiate for you and get prices locals pay
02 — Nanjing Road
Nanjing Road — Shanghai's Main Shopping Street
Metro: Line 2 — Nanjing East Road

Nanjing Road East is a 6km pedestrian street — the Times Square of Shanghai. Day and night, it's packed with shoppers.

  • International brands: Zara, H&M, Uniqlo, Nike, Adidas, Skechers
  • Department stores: Shanghai No.1 Department Store, Raffles City
  • Souvenir shops, tea houses, and traditional craft stores
  • Prices are mostly fixed — focus on international brands here
  • Best visited in the evening when it's lit up
03 — Qipu Road
Qipu Road — Shanghai's Cheapest Clothing Market
Metro: Line 4 — Hailun Road

Qipu Road (七浦路) is a sprawling wholesale clothing market — thousands of shops across multiple buildings. This is where you find incredible deals.

  • Clothes, shoes, bags, accessories — everything at wholesale prices
  • Negotiate hard: start at 30–50% of asking price, don't be shy
  • Hours: 9:00am – 7:00pm daily
  • Best on weekdays — weekends get very crowded
  • Bring cash or have WeChat Pay ready — many small vendors prefer it
  • An LFS guide can negotiate in Chinese and get you prices locals pay
04 — Xujiahui
Xujiahui — Electronics, Tech, and Brand Shopping
Metro: Line 1/9/11 — Xujiahui

Xujiahui is Shanghai's tech and electronics hub, with a concentration of brand-name stores and malls.

  • Apple Store — official store with full product range
  • Huawei flagship store — latest phones, tablets, accessories
  • Grand Gateway 66 — luxury mall with all major international brands
  • Electronics markets for components, cables, and gadgets
  • Best place for official tech purchases with warranty
05 — IFC Mall & Lujiazui
IFC Mall — Luxury Shopping With Skyline Views
Metro: Line 2 — Lujiazui

IFC Mall in Lujiazui sits at the foot of Shanghai's skyscrapers — combine luxury shopping with the famous financial district views.

  • LV, Gucci, Hermès, Chanel, Prada — all flagship stores
  • Super Brand Mall nearby — more accessible prices with mid-range brands
  • Oriental Pearl Tower, Shanghai Tower, and Jin Mao Tower visible from here
  • Great restaurants and cafes in the mall
  • Cross the river on the free Bund Sightseeing Tunnel or ferry
06 — Tianzifang
Tianzifang — Crafts, Art, and Unique Souvenirs
Metro: Line 9 — Dapuqiao

Tianzifang is a maze of narrow lanes in the French Concession, filled with independent shops, galleries, and cafes. Best place for unique gifts.

  • Hand-crafted jewelry, ceramics, calligraphy art
  • Chinese tea sets, silk scarves, embroidered items
  • Local designer clothing and accessories
  • Great cafes and snack vendors throughout
  • More authentic and less touristy than Yu Garden area
07 — What to Buy
Best Souvenirs and Items to Bring Back From Shanghai

Some items are excellent value in Shanghai and make great gifts:

  • Tea — Longjing (Dragon Well), Biluochun, Tieguanyin; buy from reputable tea shops
  • Silk products — scarves, ties, pillowcases; check for authentic silk label
  • Traditional decorations — paper cuts, cloisonné, jade ornaments
  • Notebooks & stationery — Chinese calligraphy sets and notebooks
  • Electronics — Chinese phones (Huawei, Xiaomi, OPPO) at local prices
  • Avoid fake brand goods — customs in Mongolia confiscates counterfeit items

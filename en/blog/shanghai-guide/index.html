10 Essential Tips for Your First Visit to Shanghai

May 2026 · 7 min read · LFS Shanghai
Tip 01 — Visa
Mongolians Can Enter China Visa-Free for 30 Days

Great news: Mongolian passport holders can enter China without a visa for up to 30 days. No need to visit the embassy in advance — just show up at the airport.

  • Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay
  • Fill in the customs declaration form on the plane or at arrival
  • 30-day visa-free stay — if you need longer, apply for a visa in advance
  • For stays over 30 days, you'll need a Z, X, M, or F visa depending on purpose

Tip 02 — VPN
Install a VPN Before You Leave Mongolia — This Is Urgent

In China, Google, YouTube, Instagram, Facebook, and many other services are blocked. You must install a VPN before you leave Mongolia — you cannot download VPN apps once you're in China.

  • ExpressVPN — most reliable, ~$8/month
  • NordVPN — good alternative, ~$4/month
  • Surfshark, Astrill — also work well in China
  • WeChat and Alipay work without a VPN
  • Set it up and test it in Mongolia before your trip
Tip 03 — Metro
Shanghai Metro: Cheapest and Fastest Way to Get Around

Shanghai has one of the world's best metro systems. From both airports, you can reach the city center by metro.

  • Pudong Airport (PVG) — Line 2 to city center, ~50 min, ¥9
  • Hongqiao Airport (SHA) — Line 10 or 2, ~40 min, ¥6
  • Pay with Alipay or WeChat QR code — no card or cash needed
  • Runs from ~5:30am to ~11:00pm daily

Tip 04 — Money
Shanghai Is Nearly Cashless — Set Up WeChat Pay or Alipay

Almost everywhere in Shanghai accepts payment by QR code only. Foreign cards are often not accepted.

  • Set up Alipay International with your Mongolian Visa or Mastercard
  • Set up WeChat Pay — link your Mongolian bank card
  • Exchange rate: approximately 100 CNY ≈ 50,000 MNT
  • Bring some cash (¥1,000–2,000) as backup — withdraw from ICBC or Bank of China ATMs

Tip 05 — Language
Translation Apps That Actually Work in China

Very few people in Shanghai speak English outside of tourist areas. These apps will save you:

  • Google Translate Camera — point at text, get instant translation (needs VPN)
  • Baidu Translate — works without VPN, great camera translation
  • Save key phrases offline before your trip
  • An LFS Shanghai guide speaking Mongolian removes all language barriers
Tip 06 — Transport
DiDi: Cheaper Than Taxis, Easier Than You Think

DiDi is China's Uber equivalent and is much cheaper than street taxis. You can pay with WeChat Pay or Alipay.

  • Airport to city center: approximately ¥150–200
  • Short city trips: ¥20–50
  • No need to speak Chinese — just enter the destination
  • Taxi meters are reliable too, but agree on a route first
Tip 07 — Health
Health Insurance and Medical Care in Shanghai

Travel health insurance is strongly recommended. Medical costs at international hospitals can be very high.

  • Buy travel insurance before departure — it often covers medical emergencies
  • LFS Shanghai can connect you with Mongolian-speaking doctors at Shanghai hospitals
  • Major hospitals: Huashan International, Ruijin Hospital, Shanghai East Hospital
  • Emergency: dial 120 (ambulance)

Tip 08 — Food
What to Eat in Shanghai — From ¥8 Street Food to Fine Dining

Shanghai's food scene is incredible. You can eat well for ¥10–30 per meal.

  • Xiaolongbao (soup dumplings) — Shanghai's most famous dish, ¥15–35
  • Look for 清真 (halal) signs if you need halal food
  • Yang's Dumplings (小杨生煎) — 4 dumplings for ¥8, iconic street food
  • Say "不辣" (bù là) to request no spice

Tip 09 — Safety
Shanghai Is Very Safe — But Watch Out for These Scams

Shanghai is one of the safest cities in the world. Violent crime is extremely rare. However, tourist scams do exist:

  • Tea ceremony scam — strangers invite you for "tea" that costs ¥500+. Decline politely.
  • Art student scam — "students" invite you to buy overpriced paintings
  • Keep your phone secure in crowded areas (metro, markets)
  • Carry a copy (photo) of your passport at all times
  • Street food is generally safe — stick to busy stalls with high turnover

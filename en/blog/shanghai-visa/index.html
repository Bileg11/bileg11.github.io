China Visa Guide for Mongolians — Visa-Free 30 Days in 2026

May 2026 · 5 min read · LFS Shanghai
01 — Visa-Free Entry
Mongolians Can Enter China Visa-Free for 30 Days

As of 2025, Mongolian passport holders can enter China visa-free for up to 30 days. This applies to tourism, short business trips, and transit.

  • No embassy visit required — just arrive at the port of entry
  • Passport must be valid for at least 6 months from your entry date
  • You'll receive a 30-day stay stamp upon entry
  • Applies to air, land, and sea entry points
  • Cannot be extended inside China — leave and re-enter if needed
02 — Customs Rules
What You Must Declare at Chinese Customs

Chinese customs rules are strict. Knowing what to declare prevents problems at the border:

  • Cash over $5,000 USD (or equivalent) must be declared
  • Cigarettes: limit 400 cigarettes or 50 cigars
  • Alcohol: limit 1.5 liters of spirits
  • Counterfeit goods (fake brands, pirated items) are strictly prohibited
  • Declare any gifts or commercial goods you're bringing in
  • Medications: carry a doctor's prescription for controlled substances

On the return to Mongolia: declare items worth over ¥10,000 CNY. Do not bring counterfeit goods — Mongolian customs confiscates them.

03 — When You Need a Visa
Situations That Require a Chinese Visa

The 30-day visa-free policy doesn't cover everything. You'll need a visa for:

  • Stays longer than 30 days — apply for the appropriate visa type
  • Z Visa — for working in China
  • X1/X2 Visa — for studying in China (X1 for 6+ months, X2 for under 6 months)
  • M Visa — for business/commercial activities beyond short trips
  • F Visa — for exchange or cultural visits
04 — Chinese Embassy in Ulaanbaatar
Applying for a Visa at the Chinese Embassy in UB

If you need a visa, apply at the Chinese Embassy in Ulaanbaatar:

  • Address: Olympic Street 7A, Ulaanbaatar
  • Processing time: typically 2–4 working days
  • Bring: passport, photos, completed application form, supporting documents
  • Check the embassy's official website for current requirements and hours
  • LFS Shanghai can advise on which visa type you need
⚠️ Important Warning
Overstaying Your Visa Has Serious Consequences

Overstaying your permitted days in China leads to serious consequences:

  • Heavy fines — ¥500 per day, up to ¥10,000 maximum
  • Detention before deportation
  • Multi-year entry ban — you may not be allowed back into China
  • Always know your entry date and count your days carefully
  • If you need more time, consult an immigration specialist before your visa expires
05 — Practical Tips
Practical Tips for a Smooth Entry into China

Make your arrival as smooth as possible with these preparations:

  • Have your hotel address in Chinese characters ready to show customs (download it offline)
  • Set up Alipay before your trip so you can pay immediately upon arrival
  • Install your VPN before leaving Mongolia — you cannot download it in China
  • Keep a printed or screenshot copy of your accommodation booking
  • Get a Chinese SIM at the airport immediately after customs

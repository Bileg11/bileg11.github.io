✅ Preparation Checklist

Complete Shanghai Trip Preparation Checklist — 2026

May 2026 · 7 min read · LFS Shanghai
⚠️ Urgent — Do This First
Install a VPN Before Leaving Mongolia

This is the most time-sensitive task on this list. You cannot download VPN apps inside China — the app stores are blocked. Install and test your VPN before you board the plane.

  • ExpressVPN — most reliable for China, ~$8/month
  • NordVPN — great alternative, ~$4/month
  • Surfshark or Astrill — also work well
  • Test it in Mongolia to make sure it connects
  • Download offline maps (Google Maps) while you still can

Why: Google, YouTube, Instagram, WhatsApp, Facebook, and most Western apps are blocked in China. WeChat and Alipay work without VPN.

02 — WeChat
Set Up WeChat and WeChat Pay
  • Download WeChat and register with your Mongolian phone number
  • Go to: Me → Pay → Cards → Add Card
  • Add your Mongolian Visa or Mastercard
  • Complete SMS verification
  • Test a small purchase to confirm it works
  • Easier to set up in Mongolia — can be tricky once you're in China

→ Full WeChat Pay & Alipay setup guide

03 — Alipay
Set Up Alipay International
  • Download Alipay and select International Version
  • Register with your passport number and Mongolian phone
  • Add your Visa or Mastercard to the wallet
  • Initial daily limit is $100 — increases after verification
  • Works at airports, metro, restaurants, and shops
  • Alipay QR code works at metro entry/exit gates
04 — SIM Card
Get a Chinese SIM Card at the Airport
  • Available immediately after customs at Pudong Airport
  • Carriers: China Mobile, China Unicom, China Telecom
  • Monthly data plan: ~¥100/month (bring your passport)
  • Or: buy an eSIM from your home carrier before departure
  • Mongolian roaming plans exist but are expensive for extended stays
  • Chinese SIM allows you to receive verification codes for WeChat/Alipay
05 — Translation Apps
Install Translation Apps Before Your Trip
  • Baidu Translate — camera translation works without VPN, best for Chinese
  • Microsoft Translator — works offline, download Chinese language pack
  • Google Translate works but requires VPN
  • Download offline language packs while in Mongolia (before VPN is needed)
  • Camera translation is essential for reading menus and street signs
06 — Cash
Bring Some Cash as Emergency Backup
  • WeChat/Alipay will cover 95% of payments
  • Bring ¥1,000–2,000 in cash as emergency backup
  • Withdraw from ICBC or Bank of China ATMs — these accept foreign cards
  • Exchange rate: approximately 100 CNY ≈ 50,000 MNT
  • Pudong Airport has Unionpay ATMs right after customs
  • Enable your Mongolian card for international use before departure
07 — Customs
Know the Customs Rules Both Ways

Mongolia → China (arriving):

  • Declare cash over $5,000 USD
  • Max 400 cigarettes, 1.5L alcohol
  • No fake/counterfeit branded goods

China → Mongolia (returning):

  • Declare goods worth over ¥10,000 CNY
  • Do not bring counterfeit brands — Mongolian customs confiscates them
  • Receipts for expensive items help justify value
08 — Weather
What to Pack Based on Shanghai's Weather
  • Summer (June–August): 30–38°C, very humid — light clothes, sunscreen essential
  • Winter (December–February): 0–5°C, damp cold that feels colder — bring a warm coat
  • Spring (March–May): 15–22°C — best travel season, bring a light jacket
  • Autumn (September–November): 15–25°C — second best season, comfortable temperatures
  • Shanghai can have heavy rain — a small umbrella is always useful
09 — Scam Warnings
Tourist Scams to Avoid in Shanghai
  • Tea ceremony scam: strangers invite you for tea, the bill is ¥500+. Always decline.
  • Art student scam: "students" ask you to their gallery to buy overpriced paintings
  • Fake monks: people in robes asking for donations, then demanding payment
  • Keep your phone secure in crowded metro stations and markets
  • Don't accept unsolicited "gifts" that come with strings attached
  • Book taxis through DiDi app — avoid unmarked cars outside airports

WeChat Pay & Alipay Setup in Shanghai — Using a Mongolian Card in 2026

May 2026 · 5 min read · LFS Shanghai
01 — Why You Need This
Why WeChat Pay or Alipay Is Essential in Shanghai

In Shanghai, over 90% of shops, restaurants, and services accept only QR code payments. Foreign bank cards are accepted at major hotels and some international malls — but almost nowhere else.

  • Street food, taxis, metro, shopping — all require QR code payment
  • Most foreign cards don't work at smaller merchants
  • WeChat Pay or Alipay with a Mongolian Visa/Mastercard — this is the solution
  • Set these up before leaving Mongolia — it's easier from outside China
02 — WeChat Pay
WeChat Pay International — Step by Step

Follow these steps to add your Mongolian card to WeChat Pay:

  1. Download WeChat → go to Me (bottom right)
  2. Tap Pay
  3. Tap Cards → Add Card
  4. Enter your Visa or Mastercard details
  5. Complete SMS verification sent to your Mongolian number
  6. Test with a small payment to confirm it works

Note: Per-transaction limit is $1,000 USD. Foreign cards work for payments but cannot top up a WeChat wallet balance.

Tip: Set this up in Mongolia — some steps don't work once you're inside China.

03 — Alipay
Alipay International — Step by Step

Alipay's international version is slightly easier for foreigners to set up:

  1. Download Alipay → select International Version when prompted
  2. Register with your passport number and Mongolian phone
  3. Tap Add Card → Add Payment Method
  4. Enter your Visa or Mastercard details
  5. Complete verification
  6. Daily limit starts at $100 USD — increases after full verification

Works at airports, metro stations, restaurants, convenience stores — everywhere.

04 — Mongolian Banks
Which Mongolian Bank Cards Work With WeChat & Alipay

Most major Mongolian banks' Visa and Mastercard cards are compatible:

  • Golomt Bank — Visa/Mastercard: works
  • Khan Bank (Хаан банк) — Visa/Mastercard: works
  • Trade & Development Bank (TDB) — compatible
  • State Bank — compatible
  • Before your trip: activate your card for international use at your bank
  • International transaction fees apply — check with your bank for rates
05 — Cash Backup
Getting Cash at the Airport — Emergency Reserve

Even with WeChat/Alipay set up, carry some cash for emergencies:

  • Pudong Airport (PVG) has Unionpay ATMs right after customs
  • Bank of China and ICBC ATMs accept foreign cards reliably
  • Recommended: withdraw ¥3,000–5,000 on arrival day as backup
  • Check your card's daily ATM withdrawal limit before traveling
  • Exchange rate at airport ATMs is usually better than currency exchange booths

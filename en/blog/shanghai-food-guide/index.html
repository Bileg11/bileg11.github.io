Shanghai Food Guide for Mongolians — From ¥8 Street Food to Fine Dining

May 2026 · 6 min read · LFS Shanghai
01 — Food Culture
Shanghai's Food Scene — Something for Every Budget

Shanghai is a food paradise. You can eat an excellent meal for ¥10–30, or splurge on a fine dining experience for ¥300+. The city blends traditional Shanghainese cuisine with food from every region of China.

  • Xiaolongbao (soup dumplings) — Shanghai's most iconic dish, ¥15–35
  • Shengjianbao — pan-fried pork buns, ¥8–12
  • Hong Shao Rou — red-braised pork belly, rich and sweet
  • Food halls and canteens offer incredible value at ¥10–25 per meal
02 — Budget Restaurants
Where to Eat Cheap and Well in Shanghai

You don't need to spend much to eat incredibly well in Shanghai:

  • Yang's Dumplings (小杨生煎) — 4 pan-fried buns for ¥8, lines out the door for good reason
  • Tianzifang food stalls — street food and local snacks in the French Concession
  • Nanxiang Xiaolongbao — the original xiaolongbao restaurant in Yu Garden, ¥35–60
  • University canteens (大学食堂) — open to public, meals from ¥8–15
  • Local 小吃 (xiǎo chī) — small eatery streets near residential areas
03 — Food for Mongolians
What Mongolians Typically Love in Shanghai

Some Chinese dishes are very popular with Mongolian visitors and feel somewhat familiar:

  • Hot Pot (火锅) — Haidilao is the most famous chain, great quality, cook your own food
  • Yang Guofu Malatang (杨国福麻辣烫) — pick your ingredients, ¥20–40, spicy broth
  • BBQ / Grilled Skewers (烧烤) — meat skewers on the street, very popular evening food
  • Look for 清真 (halal) signs — common in Shanghai, especially Xintiandi and Jing'an areas
  • Say "不辣" (bù là) to request no spice — very important for those who can't handle heat
04 — Street Food
Best Street Food in Shanghai

Street food is safe, delicious, and extremely cheap. Stick to busy stalls with high turnover:

  • Jianbing (煎饼) — savory crepe with egg and crispy wonton, ¥8–12, breakfast classic
  • Cong you bing (葱油饼) — scallion pancake, ¥5–8, crispy and filling
  • Tanghulu (糖葫芦) — candied fruit on a stick, ¥10, sweet snack
  • Bing tang xue li — roasted pear candy, winter specialty
  • Night markets on Yunnan Road and around People's Square are great spots
05 — Supermarkets
Grocery Shopping in Shanghai

Supermarkets are great for budget meals, snacks, and drinks:

  • RT-Mart (大润发) — large Chinese hypermarket, very cheap, great fresh food section
  • Carrefour (家乐福) — French chain with wide selection
  • 7-Eleven / FamilyMart — convenience stores on almost every corner, hot food, ¥5–15
  • Hema Fresh (盒马) — Alibaba's fresh market, excellent quality, delivery in 30 min
  • Most convenience stores have hot meals, sandwiches, and instant noodles
06 — Ordering Phrases
Essential Chinese Phrases for Ordering Food

These six phrases will get you through almost any restaurant situation:

这个
Zhège
This one (point at menu)
多少钱
Duōshao qián
How much does it cost?
不辣
Bù là
No spice please
素食
Sùshí
Vegetarian
打包
Dǎbāo
Takeaway / to go
好吃
Hǎo chī
Delicious! (compliment)
07 — Food Delivery
Food Delivery Apps in Shanghai

Food delivery in Shanghai is incredibly fast — often under 30 minutes:

  • Meituan (美团) — largest food delivery platform in China
  • Eleme (饿了么) — Alibaba's delivery service, integrated with Alipay
  • Both require WeChat Pay or Alipay — set those up first
  • Minimum order is usually ¥15–20, delivery fee ¥3–6
  • An LFS Shanghai guide can help you set up and navigate these apps

Shanghai Metro

Complete English guide — getting a card, fares, key lines, and how to get from the airport to the city

Shanghai's metro is one of the world's largest transit networks. It's the fastest, cheapest, and most straightforward way to get around the city — making it the top choice for visitors.

💳 Getting a Metro Card
Shanghai Public Transportation Card (交通卡)

Works on metro, buses, ferries, and some shops. Deposit is ¥20 and you can top it up with any amount.

  • Available at the Customer Service Center at any metro station
  • No passport required — just pay cash and get your card
  • You can get your deposit back when you leave
  • WeChat Pay and Alipay QR codes also work on the metro

📱 Riding with WeChat / Alipay

You can ride the metro without a card using WeChat Pay or Alipay.

1
Activate
WeChat: Pay → City Services → Transport → Shanghai Metro.
Alipay: Transport Card → Shanghai Metro.
2
Scan QR on entry
Hold your QR code up to the reader at the turnstile when entering.
3
Scan again on exit
Scan at the exit turnstile. The fare is calculated based on distance and charged automatically. Make sure you have enough balance in WeChat / Alipay.

🌙 After Metro Hours

Shanghai's metro usually closes between 22:30–23:30. Exact times vary by line.

🚗
DiDi — most convenient
Register with a foreign phone number. Slightly higher night fares. WeChat Pay and Alipay accepted. Airport to city center is roughly ¥150–250.
🚕
Street taxis
Hail a red taxi on the street. Metered fare. Some drivers don't speak English — having your destination in Chinese characters helps.
🚌
Night buses (夜宵公交)
¥2 fare. Key routes depart from around People's Square. Search routes on Amap.
🛵
Hellobike / Meituan bikes
Cheapest for short distances. ~¥1.5 / 30 min. Unlock by scanning a QR code.
💡 Apps & Tips
📱
MetroMan app
Offline metro map with route planning. Free on App Store and Google Play.
🗺️
Amap (高德地图)
Works far better than Google Maps in China. Shows metro, buses, and DiDi all in one place.
⏰
Rush hours
07:30–09:00 and 17:00–19:00 are very crowded. Allow extra time if traveling to or from the airport.
🧳
Traveling with large luggage
You can take the metro with big bags, but it's tough during rush hour. A DiDi taxi is more comfortable. Metro signs are in both English and Chinese, so getting lost is relatively unlikely.
